History


Prehistoric Era


Music began in Prehistoric times, to no real surprise. It was before any recorded history and is said to have been started very late in the Earth's geologic history. Native tribes invented their own folk songs, with no real origin. It may have been inspired by natural sounds and rhythms, but no one is really sure what happened back then. This was all for entertainment and tribal rituals. Instruments were most likely mainly human voices, but a 43,500 year old artifact called the "Divje Babe Flute" may be a primitive flute made out of a bear femur, but it could also be a random bone with some teeth holes in it.

The Divje Babe Flute, a possible early instrument


Ancient Era


Once a written language was developed, the prehistoric music era ended. The oldest known song was written in cuneiform 3400 years ago in Syria, with many more probably written before it. Many writings and drawings show that people started to join together to create music together and make combined sounds. Instruments were clearly depicted in many drawings, showing the importance of this in culture. Greece even created choruses and invented many new instruments, like the lyre.



Medieval


Although Medieval culture has seemed to be heavily influenced by music, the only record of an actual musical work is from 800 AD, the plainsong of the catholic church. In this time of about 800 AD, the church started effort to unify these plainsongs and chants or supress the ones that didn;t support Gregorian liturgy. Polyphonic music (texture of music that blends many aspects of the way it is sung) was sung for the first time, and music notation resurfaced after being gone for a long time. Schools for music were starting to appear after 1100. Several music ideas were starting to surface that helped form the Renaissance Era of music, like troubadours, composers of Old Occitan lyric poetry. In the 13th century, sacred music was developed, and so did Geisslerlieder, or the music of wandering bands. In the 14th century, a style of music called ars nova was formed, which is a late Medieval Style that closely relates to the Renaissance of Music.
